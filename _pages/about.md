I am an incoming Ph.D. student in the [Department of Industrial Engineering and Operations Research (IEOR)](https://ieor.berkeley.edu) at the [University of California, Berkeley](https://www.berkeley.edu), starting in Fall 2026. I am currently a senior student in the [School of Intelligence Science and Technology](https://is.nju.edu.cn/is_en/main.htm) at [Nanjing University](https://www.nju.edu.cn/en/).

Previously, I studied as an international student in the [International Guest Student Program](https://www.lps.upenn.edu/non-degree-programs/igsp) at the [University of Pennsylvania](https://www.upenn.edu/) in Fall 2024, and participated in the [Global Education Advancement and Research Seminar Program](https://gti.ncsu.edu/gears/) at [North Carolina State University](https://www.ncsu.edu) in Summer 2024.

## Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of operations research and machine learning, with a focus on sequential decision-making and learning theory. Specifically, I study how to design and analyze algorithms for multi-agent systems, including multi-agent multi-armed bandit problems and multi-agent preference learning (such as RLHF for LLM alignment), across both online and offline settings. Ultimately, I aim to address the challenges posed by imperfect environments arising from limited or biased data, and to develop algorithms that better capture the complexities of real-world applications.
